Chicken Biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish, mainly popular in South Asia. It is made with rice, some type of meat and spices.
To cater to vegetarians, in some cases, it is prepared by substituting vegetables or paneer for the meat. Sometimes eggs and/or potatoes are also added.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:
  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani Masala:

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (for garnishing)
  • Lemon juice to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and coat well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the green cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, and salt. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water and cook until the rice is 70-80% done. The grains should be soft but still have a bite to them. Drain the rice and set aside.

  3. Cook the Chicken: In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Add cumin seeds and green chilies. Sauté for a minute. Add the chopped tomatoes, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook on medium heat until the chicken is cooked through and the oil separates from the gravy. Stir in the garam masala powder. Add chopped coriander and mint leaves, and mix well.

  4. Layer the Biryani: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, layer half of the cooked rice at the bottom. Top with the cooked chicken and its masala. Add the remaining rice on top of the chicken layer. Warm the milk and dissolve the saffron strands in it (if using). Drizzle this saffron milk over the rice. Sprinkle fried onions and lemon juice on top.

  5. Dum Cooking (Steam Cooking): Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid is not tight, you can seal the edges with dough to ensure no steam escapes. Place the pot on a low flame and let it cook for about 20-25 minutes. You can also place the pot on a griddle (tawa) to prevent direct heat from burning the biryani.

  6. Serve: Once done, gently fluff the rice with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice grains. Serve hot with raita, salad, and pickle.


Chicken Karahi

Chicken karahi, or kadai chicken, is a chicken dish from South Asia. It is known as gosht karahi when prepared with goat or lamb meat instead of chicken. It is noted for its spicy taste and is notable in South Asian Cuisine.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces (with bone-in for more flavor)
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or cooking oil
  • 2 large onions, finely sliced
  • 2-3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
  • Fresh ginger, julienned for garnish
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Chicken:

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large karahi or heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and allow them to splutter.
  3. Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

2. Cook the Chicken:

  1. Add ginger-garlic paste to the pan and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes are softened and oil starts to separate from the masala.

3. Add Chicken:

  1. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and mix well with the masala.
  2. Cover and cook on medium-low heat for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is tender and cooked through. The chicken will release its own juices during cooking.

4. Finish the Dish:

  1. Once the chicken is cooked, uncover the pan and increase the heat to high.
  2. Stir-fry the chicken for a few minutes to evaporate any excess liquid and to allow the masala to coat the chicken pieces well.
  3. Sprinkle garam masala powder over the chicken and mix thoroughly.
  4. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves and julienned ginger.

5. Serve:

  1. Transfer Chicken Karahi to a serving dish.
  2. Serve hot with naan bread, roti, or steamed rice.
  3. Garnish with lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the chicken before eating.

Enjoy your flavorful Chicken Karahi! Adjust the spiciness according to your preference by varying the amount of red chili powder and green chilies.
