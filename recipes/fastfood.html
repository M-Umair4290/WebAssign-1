Zinger Burger

A Zinger Burger is a fried chicken recipe with a spicy twist. Hot fried chicken is sandwiched between a sesame bun together
with lettuce and a creamy dressing for a classic chicken burger with a hint of spice.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless chicken breasts, cut in half horizontally to make 4 fillets
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (like Sriracha or Tabasco)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder

For the Coating:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder

For Assembly:

  • 4 burger buns, toasted
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced onions (optional)
  • Mayonnaise or spicy mayo
  • Pickles

For Frying:

  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

1. Marinate the Chicken:

  1. In a bowl, combine buttermilk, hot sauce, salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  2. Add chicken fillets to the marinade, making sure they are fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (preferably longer, up to overnight).

2. Prepare the Coating:

  1. In a shallow dish, combine flour, cornstarch, salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder. Mix well.

3. Coat the Chicken:

  1. Remove chicken fillets from the marinade, allowing excess marinade to drip off.
  2. Dredge each fillet in the seasoned flour mixture, ensuring they are evenly coated. Press the flour mixture onto the chicken to adhere well.
  3. Place the coated fillets on a plate or wire rack and let them rest for about 10-15 minutes. This helps the coating to set.

4. Fry the Chicken:

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer or a heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Carefully place the coated chicken fillets into the hot oil, a few at a time, depending on the size of your fryer or pot. Do not overcrowd.
  3. Fry the chicken fillets for about 5-6 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (75°C).
  4. Remove the chicken fillets from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

5. Assemble the Burger:

  1. Toast the burger buns lightly on a griddle or in a toaster.
  2. Spread mayonnaise or spicy mayo on the bottom half of each bun.
  3. Layer with lettuce leaves, sliced tomatoes, and onions (if using).
  4. Place a fried chicken fillet on top of the vegetables.
  5. Add pickles on top of the chicken fillet.
  6. Close the burger with the top half of the bun.

6. Serve:

  1. Serve the Chicken Zinger Burgers immediately while still warm.
  2. Enjoy with fries or your favorite side dishes.


Fried Chicken

Fried chicken, also known as Southern fried chicken, is a dish consisting of chicken pieces that have been coated with seasoned flour or batter and pan-fried, deep fried, pressure fried, or air fried.
The breading adds a crisp coating or crust to the exterior of the chicken while retaining juices in the meat. Broiler chickens are most commonly used.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

For the Chicken:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 lbs), cut into pieces (or use your preferred chicken parts like drumsticks, thighs, breasts)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional, for added color and flavor)

For the Marinade (optional, for extra flavor):

  • 1 cup buttermilk (or substitute with plain yogurt or milk mixed with 1 tablespoon vinegar or lemon juice)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

For the Coating:

  • 1.5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste, for heat)
  • Oil, for frying (such as vegetable oil or canola oil)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Chicken:

  1. If using the marinade, combine buttermilk (or substitute), salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper in a bowl.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (preferably longer, up to overnight).

2. Prepare the Coating:

  1. In a shallow dish or bowl, mix together flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper.

3. Coat the Chicken:

  1. Remove chicken pieces from the marinade (if using) and allow any excess to drip off.
  2. Dredge each piece of chicken in the seasoned flour mixture, pressing the flour onto the chicken to ensure it adheres well. Shake off any excess flour.

4. Fry the Chicken:

  1. Heat oil in a large, deep skillet or pot over medium-high heat. The oil should be about 350°F (175°C) for frying.
  2. Carefully place chicken pieces in the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding the pan. Fry in batches if necessary.
  3. Fry each piece for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (75°C) for chicken to be fully cooked.
  4. Remove fried chicken pieces with tongs or a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Repeat with remaining chicken pieces.

5. Serve:

  1. Transfer the fried chicken to a serving platter.
  2. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.
  3. Enjoy your crispy and delicious homemade Fried Chicken with your favorite sides like mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits!

This recipe yields perfectly crispy and juicy fried chicken that's sure to be a hit at any meal. Adjust the seasonings to your taste preferences and enjoy!
